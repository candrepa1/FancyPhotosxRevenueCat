Cross-Platform Subscription Management

Manage your subscriptions across iOS and Android with a unified setup.

General Setup

Steps to apply to both platforms:

  1. Connect your stores

    Connect the App Store and Google Play Store to your RevenueCat project. Configure Apple Server Notifications and Real-Time Developer Notifications so RevenueCat is notified of purchase and subscription events as they happen.

  2. Create and import products

    Define your product IDs, pricing, and billing periods in each store, then automatically import them into RevenueCat.

  3. Configure your product catalog

    Set up your product catalog in RevenueCat:

    • Entitlements: define what the user is entitled to use (e.g. "pro", "max" tiers).
    • Offerings: the set of products presented to the user. Group products into offerings (e.g. the Pro tier has "monthly" and "yearly" products).
    • Products: a mapping of the products defined in your stores, tied to offerings and entitlements.

  4. Install the SDK

    Install the RevenueCat SDK for iOS and Android.

iOS Migration

Since you already have an iOS app with existing subscriptions, there are a couple of extra steps:

  1. Track new purchases

    Toggle "Track new purchases" in the RevenueCat app settings so new transactions are automatically captured.

  2. Import historical purchases

    Migrate your existing receipts or purchase tokens from your server to RevenueCat via the POST /receipts endpoint.

Android

Since the Android app is new and there are no historical purchases, no extra steps are needed beyond the general setup.

FAQs
Can a user share their subscription across platforms?

Yes, as long as the user has the same App User ID across all platforms, their subscription and entitlements will be shared automatically.

FAQs
What happens to existing subscribers when the schedule expires?

Their entitlement/access stays active regardless of the schedule.

Can this be toggled manually outside the schedule?

Yes, you can make it live/inactive from the RevenueCat dashboard at any time.

How quickly does the change propagate?

Next time the SDK fetches offerings, typically when the app is next opened or brought back to the foreground.

What if I have other offerings with different paywalls?

You can retrieve the right offering via placements, the current offering, or by querying for a specific offering by name.

Fraud Prevention

Given the cost of your AI compute, here's how we can help minimize unauthorized access to those features.

Here's a high-level view of the fraud prevention strategies covered in this section:

Receipt Validation

It is one of RevenueCat's implementation responsibilities to verify receipts directly with the app stores, assuring transaction integrity. This comes baked into RevenueCat's backend so there's no action needed from your side.

Server-Side Feature Gating

You can verify user entitlements on the client, but there's always a risk that requests are tampered with, causing fraudulent users to bypass these checks.

In order to add an extra protective layer, we recommend adding an entitlement check on the server running your AI compute, using RevenueCat's /GET active user entitlements endpoint and grant access accordingly.

Trusted Entitlements

Man-in-the-Middle (MiTM) attacks can tamper with response content between your app and RevenueCat's servers. For that, RevenueCat has built Trusted Entitlements which verifies the integrity of these responses on the client side.

After the verification, RevenueCat provides a verificationResult, which you can use to make your access logic more robust:

  • .notRequested — No verification was done (verification not enabled in configuration, or iOS < 13.0)
  • .verified — Entitlements were verified with RevenueCat's server
  • .verifiedOnDevice — Entitlements were created and verified on device through StoreKit 2
  • .failed — Verification failed, possibly due to a MiTM attack

Refund Handling

App Store
Prerequisite: Apple Server Notifications must be configured.

There's a RevenueCat configuration to handle refund requests automatically. Given the cost of the AI features, we'd suggest starting with "Always prefer declining refunds" and adjusting based on your refund patterns over time.

RevenueCat will automatically send the user's consumption data to Apple directly in order for them to make informed refund decisions.

Just remember to update the terms and conditions of your app to obtain consent from your customers to share their consumption data with Apple.

Google Play Store

We recommend handling refunds through the RevenueCat dashboard or the POST /revoke endpoint to ensure access is properly revoked alongside the refund.
